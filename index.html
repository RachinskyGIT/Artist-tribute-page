Alona Barkov

Artist and costume designer

Biography

Alona Barkov was born in 1995 in Moscow, Russia and comes from a family of artists. Continuing the family tradition, in 2014 Alona went to study at VGIK University, from which she graduated with honors in 2020.

During her studies, Alona created a number of outstanding student projects: painting, graphics, costume models. Her projects have received numerous awards at various competitions and festivals.

After graduating from university, for two years, Alona worked in cinema as an assistant costume designer and wrote commissioned works.

In the spring of 2022, after the start of Russia's criminal invasion of Ukraine, Alona repatriated to Israel, not wanting to continue living under the Russian regime.

In Israel, Alona began working on a new series of paintings in the field of figurative art. In her work, she was inspired by the artists of the modern American school, including Mark Tennant, Doran Langberg, Jenna Gribbon and others.

Israel greatly influenced her worldview. Alona was impressed by this small country, where an incredible number of nationalities and cultures intersect, where a large number of young people live, adhere to the casual style and love to hang out. Alona is inspired by the image of a casual Tel Aviv, where the spirit of freedom lives.

Now in her works, Alona is experimenting with composition, using sharp angles and bright accents. The combination of contrasts of light and shadow, artificial colors help her create images of young people who want to attract attention, thereby making their statement visible to society.

Alona participates in various exhibitions of artists, and is always open to cooperation and new experiences.
